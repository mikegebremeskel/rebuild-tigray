## What they do

The Habesha Arts Collective supports Tigrayan artists in the diaspora and creates programming that makes Tigrayan cultural heritage visible — to the community itself and to the broader public. The collective was founded in 2020 in the months following the outbreak of the war, born out of the recognition that culture is not separate from survival: it is what a people carries forward when everything else is under threat.

The collective runs artist residencies, produces a documentary series on the diaspora experience, organizes cultural exhibitions and performances, and runs a Tigrinya language program for children of diaspora families. It also supports a growing archive of Tigrayan music, oral history, and visual art.

## Their approach

The collective takes a long view. Political conditions change; cultural knowledge, once lost, does not come back easily. The work of the collective is to ensure that what Tigrayans have created — in music, in language, in visual culture, in oral tradition — is not lost to displacement, assimilation, or the focused destruction that was part of the recent conflict.

The collective also believes that art is a form of advocacy. It partners with advocacy organizations to use documentary film and visual storytelling as tools for building understanding of the situation in Tigray.

## Key focus areas

- Tigrayan artist residencies and commissions
- Documentary film production on the diaspora experience
- Tigrinya language and cultural education for diaspora youth
- Archive of Tigrayan music, oral history, and visual art
- Cultural exhibitions and performances in diaspora cities
