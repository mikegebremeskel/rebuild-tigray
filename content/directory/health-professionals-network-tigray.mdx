## What they do

The Health Professionals Network for Tigray (HPNT) brings together Tigrayan physicians, nurses, public health professionals, and allied health workers based in the diaspora. The network was formed in direct response to the systematic destruction of Tigray's health infrastructure during the 2020–2022 genocide, which left hospitals destroyed, staff displaced, and supply chains severed.

The network operates on two tracks: immediate crisis response and long-term health system rebuilding. During the acute phase of the conflict, members coordinated the donation and delivery of medical supplies, provided remote clinical consultation to overwhelmed staff on the ground, and worked with international organizations to document health system damage.

## Their approach

The HPNT takes a professional-network approach, not a charity model. Members apply their clinical and public health expertise directly to problems in Tigray — through training, telemedicine consultations, policy advocacy, and supply chain coordination. The goal is to transfer knowledge and build capacity, not to create dependency.

The network also serves as a professional home for Tigrayan health workers in the diaspora: a place to find colleagues, coordinate efforts, and stay connected to the work happening in Tigray even from thousands of miles away.

## Key focus areas

- Medical supply coordination and donation
- Telemedicine consultation for healthcare providers in Tigray
- Training and professional development support
- Health systems documentation and recovery planning
- Advocacy on healthcare access and protection of medical facilities
