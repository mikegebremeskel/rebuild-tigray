## What they do

The Tigray Education Fund works on the educational recovery of Tigray, where the 2020–2022 war caused widespread destruction of schools and the displacement of hundreds of thousands of students. The fund operates on two tracks: immediate support for students whose education was interrupted, and long-term investment in rebuilding the institutions and systems that education depends on.

Scholarship programs support displaced university students who lost academic years or cannot afford to continue. School reconstruction grants go directly to communities identifying their most urgent rebuilding needs. And teacher support programs recognize that schools without qualified, paid, present teachers are shells.

## Their approach

The fund takes an education-systems view, not just a school-buildings view. Physical reconstruction matters, but so does curriculum continuity, teacher retention, and the psychosocial support that allows children who have witnessed violence to learn. The fund's partners on the ground include teachers, school directors, and local education officials who understand the specific conditions in each community.

## Key focus areas

- Scholarships for displaced university and secondary students
- School reconstruction grants to communities in Tigray
- Teacher retention and professional support
- Psychosocial and learning support for conflict-affected children
- Advocacy for education funding in international reconstruction plans
