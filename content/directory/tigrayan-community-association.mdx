## What they do

The Tigrayan Community Association (TCA) is a network of local chapters across the United States, Canada, and Europe, each serving as a gathering point for Tigrayans in the diaspora. The work is unglamorous and indispensable: helping newly arrived families navigate housing and employment, organizing community gatherings, and maintaining the social fabric that makes diaspora life sustainable.

When the war began in November 2020, TCA chapters became emergency coordination hubs — connecting families separated by the conflict, organizing financial support for those with family on the ground, and providing a physical space for people to grieve, organize, and act together.

## Their approach

TCA operates on the principle that community infrastructure is not a luxury — it is the foundation on which all other work rests. Without connected, supported communities, the diaspora fragments. The harder advocacy, fundraising, and rebuilding work depends on people who feel held by something larger than themselves.

The association runs regular town halls, cultural events, and mentorship programs, alongside the more immediate work of mutual aid and emergency support.

## Key focus areas

- Local chapter programming across diaspora cities
- Mutual aid and emergency family support
- Cultural events, language preservation, and youth programming
- Community grief and healing spaces
- Connection between newly arrived Tigrayans and established diaspora networks
