## What they do

The Tigrayan Women's Network was formed in direct response to the documented use of sexual violence as a weapon during the 2020–2022 conflict, and to the broader recognition that women bear disproportionate burdens in conflict and recovery — as caregivers, as survivors, and as organizers who are often invisible in formal leadership.

The network works across several areas: survivor support and advocacy, documentation of gender-based violence for international accountability processes, policy advocacy that centers women's voices in reconstruction planning, and programming in the diaspora that supports Tigrayan women who are themselves often navigating trauma and displacement.

## Their approach

The network is women-led at every level. It takes as its starting point that women are not just victims of this conflict but central actors in its resolution and in the rebuilding that follows. The network brings together women in the diaspora with women's organizations operating inside Tigray, and works to ensure that international attention to gender-based violence translates into concrete accountability and survivor support.

## Key focus areas

- Survivor support and legal advocacy
- Documentation of gender-based violence for accountability processes
- Policy advocacy centering women in reconstruction planning
- Mental health and healing programs for diaspora women
- Economic support for women-headed households in Tigray
