## What they do

The Tigrayan Youth Sports Federation organizes football (soccer), basketball, and volleyball leagues and tournaments across North America, bringing together Tigrayan youth from diaspora communities in dozens of cities. The federation runs an annual tournament that has become one of the largest regular gatherings of Tigrayans in the diaspora — part athletic competition, part reunion, part affirmation that there is a community here.

The sports programs are explicitly about more than athletics. They are about creating spaces where young Tigrayans — many of them born in the diaspora, some newly arrived — can find peers, develop identity, and stay connected to a community and culture that is otherwise dispersed across a continent.

## Their approach

The federation understands sports as community infrastructure. The tournaments and leagues create the recurring occasions for Tigrayans to be together — which then creates the relational substrate on which organizing, advocacy, and mutual support become possible. The federation also runs leadership development programs for young adults, many of whom go on to leadership roles in other diaspora organizations.

## Key focus areas

- Annual multi-city tournament and regional leagues
- Year-round youth programming and club sports
- Leadership development for young Tigrayan adults
- Connection between diaspora youth and Tigrayan cultural identity
- Fundraising tournaments for humanitarian and education programs
