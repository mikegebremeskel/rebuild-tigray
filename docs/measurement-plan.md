# Measurement Plan

*What we measure — and deliberately ignore — at each stage. The right metric depends on the stage; measuring launch metrics during discovery is just noise. Last updated 2026-06.*

> **Principle:** Measure to learn, not to look busy. The instrument should match the question the stage is asking.

A common mistake is to instrument analytics early because it *feels* rigorous. On a pre-launch product with no audience, a dashboard precisely measures zero. This map keeps me from optimizing the wrong thing.

---

## Stage 1 — Discovery *(where we are now)*

**Goal:** Learn what the diaspora actually needs, and let that shape what this becomes. I'm validating the *problem* and listening for the shape of the answer — not yet validating a finished solution.

**Instrument:** A [Typeform](https://www.typeform.com/) for open feedback, plus direct conversations with diaspora members and organizations. Manual and qualitative, by design.

**What I'm watching (by hand):**
- **Themes over volume** — what people say is missing, what they'd actually use, which needs recur
- **Problem resonance** — does the core problem (fragmentation, invisibility of the work) land, or do people point somewhere else?
- **Expressions of want** — "I'd use this," "feature my org," "can it also do X"
- **Organic pull** — anyone referring me to another org or person unprompted (the strongest early signal there is)

**What I deliberately ignore:** Pageviews, traffic, session counts, any aggregate engagement metric. No analytics dashboard — N is too small, and one real conversation is richer than a thousand anonymous visits at this scale.

**Move on when:** A clear, repeated pattern emerges about what this should be, and there's enough inbound interest to justify building it for real. This is a *signal*, not a date — I'm intentionally not rushing it.

## Stage 2 — Launch

**Goal:** Prove the core loop works — people discover organizations and act on it.

**Instrument:** Real content + basic product analytics on the discovery funnel.

**What I'd measure:**
- **Coverage** — # of verified organizations listed
- **Discovery** — did a visitor reach an org detail page? (did they *find* someone?)
- **Intent to connect** — outbound clicks to org websites
- **Inbound pull** — organizations asking to be listed
- **Return visits**

**What I'd ignore:** Absolute traffic in isolation. The question is whether the funnel *converts*, not how big the top of it is.

**Move on when:** The discovery loop converts consistently and organizations are asking their way in.

## Stage 3 — Scale

**Goal:** The network compounds — visibility turns into connection turns into collaboration (the core bet).

**Lagging indicators:** Weekly active contributors, people reporting they connected *via* the site, engagement among second-generation diaspora, breadth of coverage across organizations and geographies.

---

## Current state (honest)

We are in **Stage 1, by choice.** The Typeform is live and gathering feedback; I'm seeking input from real people before shaping the next version. No product analytics are instrumented — and at this stage, that's correct, not a gap. The ultimate shape of the product is an open question I'm deliberately letting the feedback answer.
